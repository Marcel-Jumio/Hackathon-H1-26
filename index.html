1 Problem & Impact

What problem are you solving?

Cold identity demos don't land. Prospects can't picture the Jumio WebSDK inside their own onboarding flow, and a real branded integration takes an engineering team days. That friction kills momentum in the earliest, most fragile part of a deal.

Who is the target user?

Jumio field teams — AEs, solutions engineers and BDRs running first-touch outreach, discovery and demos. Secondary: the prospect on the other side, who sees their own brand reflected back on a live Jumio flow.

Why is this problem important?

Attention is the scarcest asset in the field. A generic demo says "here's our product." A microsite skinned as the prospect's own onboarding says "here's you, with friction removed" — it shifts the conversation from features to fit, and frames Jumio as identity intelligence built into their journey rather than a bolt-on check.

How do users currently solve this?

Generic, unbranded demo flows, or slide mockups of "what it could look like." A truly branded demo today requires a real integration — days of engineering the field can't requisition for a single conversation.

What measurable impact could this create?ASSUMPTION

Branded demo turnaround cut from days of engineering to under a minute, self-serve. Leading indicators to track post-launch: demo-to-opportunity conversion, first-touch engagement, and SE hours saved per demo. Confirm the specific metric the team will commit to.

2 Solution & Innovation

Your solution in one sentence?

The Jumio SmartDemo Tool captures a prospect's brand from a URL, auto-themes the Jumio WebSDK to match, and embeds it in a shareable, brand-skinned onboarding microsite — in seconds.

What makes it unique or innovative?

It drives customization channels 1–4 of the WebSDK (342 design tokens, ~45 template slots, theme mode) from a single generated profile. A normal integration only gets channel 5: three server-side branding knobs. The differentiator is "three knobs vs. a full repaint."

Why is it better than alternatives?

It's real, not a mockup — the embedded SDK is genuinely token-themeable and runs a live verification flow. And it's self-serve for the field: no engineering ticket, no integration sprint.

Most creative aspect?

The ramp generator: one brand hex color expands into a 7-step lightness ramp (color-primary1..7) that drives every interactive state of the SDK. One color in, a coherent themed product out.

If you had another month?ASSUMPTION

Harden the crawler for full-auto across arbitrary sites, add dark-mode token generation, expand journey verticals beyond banking, and wire one-click publish + QR sharing into a hosted gallery of saved demos. Confirm the team's actual roadmap.

3 Technical Execution

Walk us through the architecture.

A single contract — brand-profile.json — connects every component. Harvest produces it; the theming engine maps it to --jumio-sdk-* CSS variables + logo/font templates; the microsite injects that styling and mounts the SDK at the verification step; the studio UI lets a human review and edit the profile. Full data-flow diagram is in the How We Built It tab.

Technologies, frameworks, APIs?ASSUMPTION

Jumio WebSDK (token + template theming), JSON Schema (Draft 2020-12) for the contract, vanilla HTML/CSS/JS for the microsite shell, and Playwright for the crawler/extractor track. Confirm the final stack actually used.

Most technically challenging part?ASSUMPTION

Generating a brand-faithful, accessible color ramp from a single primary hex, and injecting theming through the SDK's Shadow DOM cleanly. Confirm with the engine owner.

Reliability / scalability / security?

An "always-green" design: the manual path always works, so a flaky crawl never breaks the demo. Hero-brand profiles are pre-cached so the live demo never depends on a network crawl. Theming is pure CSS-variable injection — no SDK fork, no build step.

Trade-offs you made?

Light mode only for the hackathon (dark-mode token twins deferred). Don't clone the prospect's site — a clean brand-skinned 3-step stepper reads as "their flow" and is achievable in scope. Contract-first over speed-first, to unblock parallel work.

How it worksOne artifact ties it all together

Everything hangs off a single file — brand-profile.json. The harvesting team produces it; the theming engine consumes it; the microsite and UI read it. Lock this schema in the first two hours and every team builds in parallel against mock data.

The contract — brand-profile.json

// The single source of truth. Harvest fills it, the engine themes from it.
{
  "brand": {
    "name": "Acme Bank",
    "colors": { "primary": "#0a7d33", "onPrimary": "#fff",
                 "text": "#111", "pageBackground": "#fff" },
    "fontFamily": "Inter",
    "logo": "<url|base64>"
  },
  "journey": { "steps": ["Create account", "Verify identity", "Done"],
              "idvStepIndex": 1 },
  "sdk": { "workflow": "id+selfie", "locale": "en" }
}

The mapping layer (brand → SDK) reuses what the WebSDK already supports: colors.primary → a generated lightness ramp on --jumio-sdk-color-primary1..7; logo → the <template id="jumio-logotype-*"> slots; fontFamily → a font <link> + --jumio-sdk-font-family.
